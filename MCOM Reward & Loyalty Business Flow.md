* MCOM Central already knows the business.
* Audit already knows the business problems.
* Recommendations already know what the business needs.
* Reward & Loyalty simply activates the customer retention tools required for that business.

The business should almost never be answering onboarding questions again.

The flow should feel like:

> "We already understand your business. Here are the loyalty tools we recommend for you."

↓

Business opens Reward & Loyalty

↓

Welcome Screen

↓

Recommended Programme Displayed

↓

Business selects template

↓

Template activates:

* Points
* Stamps

* Gift Cards
* Matching Points

↓

Campaigns activated

↓

Customer wallet goes live

↓

Business tracks performance

That flow is much closer to Henry's vision because Reward & Loyalty becomes an **activation platform**, not an onboarding platform. It assumes the ecosystem already knows the business and simply helps the business deploy customer retention mechanisms quickly using admin-controlled templates rather than forcing every business to build loyalty programmes from scratch.
